Internet Collaborations

Collaboration is an area that has sky rocketed since the beginning of the pandemic, where companies were forced with a new challenges of remote working and had to figure out to make it as collaborative as the office environment.



Collaboration tools such as Microsoft Teams, GoToMeeting, Skype, Nifty etc., made communication and production more efficient for teams working together or sharing ideas from different locations. It is a way for organizations to grow and improve project efficiency whilst facilitating better connection and communication. (Glossop, 2023)

There are couple of online collaborations that have to meet a team’s specific requirements, such as:

  1. Security systems are key during online collaboration, such as encryption, usernames and passwords and two-step verification and biometric identification are important to have in place in order to protect client’s information.



  2. Online collaboration ensure seamless file sharing.


  3. Interactions are very helpful for efficient collaboration, being able to message individual team members or groups on the platform in order to share and ideas and information.


  4. Calendar views are great way for companies and employees to track meetings and projects in order to meet their targets.




  5. Notification systems allows team members to perform tasks more efficiently and keep up with the updates and changes.


  6. Cloud file storage feature is a place where team members keep files, secure and accessible. Allowing everyone to access regardless of their location.




Overall, online collaboration improves staff productivity, helps to save money on office supplies, and offers less dependence on emails.


(Indeed-Editorial-Team, 2023)

e-Commerce

e-Commerce is an electronic system that enables customers to view and buy products online. These products may be physical, digital or services such as subscriptions.



Businesses that take up the e-commerce platform have a:
  • Low financial cost, are able to sell internationally, target customers based to their browsing history.

  • E-Commerce business users have a potential income thats open 24/7 as anyone from any location can access it.

  • They are able to display best-sellers to prove themselves and make it easier to show off products to customers.

  • Including customers to email marketing to keep them informed.

  • Personalization of customers emails through use of their subscription names.

    • Sending emails if the customer left item in the cart and showing them an ad for that product.
  • Fewer employees are needed in an on-line business than in a retails location.
    • Capitalize on impulse buying.
(Ferreira, 2023)
